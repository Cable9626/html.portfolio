LAMBORGHINI AVENTADOR

Overview: What the Aventador Is

The Lamborghini Aventador is a flagship Italian supercar produced from 2011 to around 2022, known for its dramatic styling, naturally aspirated V12 engine, and blistering performance. It embodies Lamborghini’s classic philosophy of extreme power, sharp design, and driver-focused experience.

Engine & Performance

  • Engine: 6.5-litre naturally aspirated V12 — iconic, high-revving, and dramatic.
  • Horsepower: Varies by version — roughly 700 hp in early models up to ~780 hp in the final Ultimae.
  • Torque: ~690–720 Nm depending on variant.
  • 0–100 km/h (0–62 mph): Around 2.7–2.9 seconds
  • Top Speed: About 350 km/h / 217 mph (electronically limited).

Chassis & Tech

  • Drivetrain: AWD (All-Wheel Drive) for traction and stability.
  • Transmission: 7-speed ISR single-clutch.
  • Chassis: Carbon-fibre monocoque for stiffness and low weight.
  • Suspension: Pushrod or magneto-rheological dampers on later variants.

Notable Versions

  • Aventador SV (SuperVeloce)

    A lighter, more track-focused model with sharper aero and more punch.

  • Aventador SVJ(Pictured)

    Top-tier performance with ~770 hp V12, advanced active aerodynamics (ALA system), and a Nürburgring lap record for production cars in its era.

  • Aventador LP700-4

    The original production version that launched the line — spectacular V12 performance and dramatic design.

    Aventador S

    • More power (~740 hp), improved aerodynamics, rear-wheel steering, and enhanced handling.
